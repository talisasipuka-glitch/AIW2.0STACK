CHOSEN NICHE: personal-injury-lawyers

Why this one over the others:
- Highest per-case value of the three niches ($6,600 to $11,500 in attorney fees per signed case) makes the student's fee easy for a firm owner to justify against a single converted case.
- The trust-stack formula for this niche is unusually clear and repeatable (no-fee-unless-we-win messaging, settlement-dollar proof, named case-type landing pages, a short violation-count contact form), which lets the student build fast and consistently across clients.
- It is a US-focused, fully remote niche the student can scale from South Africa without local connections, and represents the steepest growth and skill challenge of the three, which the student wants for this stage.

Niche customer reality check:
- They research before buying: yes. End customers compare firms on legal directories (Justia, Super Lawyers), Google Business Profile reviews, and case-result pages before calling, and often ask in Reddit/Facebook accident-recovery communities about which firm to trust.
- Average ticket: $6,600 to $11,500 in attorney fees per signed case (1/3 contingency on a typical $20,000 to $35,000 settlement).
- They hang out at: legal directories (Justia, Super Lawyers, Avvo), Google Business Profile / Google Maps listings, Facebook accident and injury-support groups, and car-accident-related subreddits.
- A $5 to 15k website ROI math: at 4-5 qualified leads a month and a 1-in-4-to-5 sign rate, the firm signs roughly 1 new case a month from the site. One signed case is worth $6,600 to $11,500 in fees, so a $5,000 to $15,000 website pays for itself in 1 to 2 months and keeps paying every month after that.
