## Decision moments (inferred from review content and firm's own framing)

- The end customer is almost always reviewing AFTER an accident (car crash is the dominant scenario in this sample) — they are not researching PI lawyers as a hobby; they are in an acute, stressful, often physically painful situation.
- Several reviews mention being "recommended" to the firm — word-of-mouth/referral is a real channel, but the firm's own marketing ("Your road to justice starts with a single call," free consultations) targets the cold-search moment: someone with no referral, searching "personal injury lawyer near me" right after an accident.
- The firm's own social posts model the exact fears they're addressing: "Legal issues don't wait for a 'good time' to happen" (urgency/no time to research) and "we offer free initial consultations to help you understand your rights" (fear of cost/commitment before understanding options).
- The "what to do after a fender bender" post (check for injuries, call police, take photos, then call us) shows the firm positioning itself as part of the immediate-aftermath checklist — a content/SEO opportunity (see Sub-task 6).

## Top 5 fears (synthesized from review language + firm's own framing)

1. **Being ignored or left in the dark** — "kept me updated," "answered any questions," "transparent communication" are the most repeated positives, implying their absence (silence, unreturned calls) is the default fear.
2. **Not getting enough compensation / being lowballed** — "fight for you to get the best quote back," "money back to replace our totaled vehicle" — fear that without a strong advocate, the insurance payout won't cover real costs (medical bills, lost wages, vehicle replacement).
3. **Not understanding the legal process** — "explained every aspect of the process," "help you understand your rights and your options" — fear of navigating an unfamiliar, high-stakes process alone while injured.
4. **Cost of hiring a lawyer before knowing if it's worth it** — addressed directly by "free initial consultations," implying this is a real barrier to first contact.
5. **Picking the wrong firm in a sea of identical-sounding ads** — with 12,572 PI attorneys in New York and 6,334 in Texas alone (Sub-task 1, iLawyer Marketing data), the end customer faces an overwhelming, low-differentiation choice at the exact moment they are least equipped (injured, stressed) to evaluate it carefully.
