## Top 10 CTAs and phrasing pattern

1. "Free Case Evaluation" (dolmanlaw.com, morganandmorgan.com) — the single most common CTA across the niche, appears on nearly every homepage.
2. "Request a Free Case Review" (omarochoalaw.com) — same mechanic, "Review" instead of "Evaluation."
3. "Get your free case review" (omarochoalaw.com, repeated as a secondary CTA mid-page)
4. "Contact Us / Call Us" paired CTA (omarochoalaw.com) — phone-first option sits next to the form CTA.
5. "Need help right away? Call us! (956) 253-3121" (omarochoalaw.com) — **pattern: urgency framing + direct phone number inline in body copy, not just in the header**
6. "Submit your claim" (morganandmorgan.com, Step 1 of a 3-step "How it works" CTA flow)
7. "Don't settle for less than you deserve!" (omarochoalaw.com) — **pattern: CTA framed as a warning against the alternative (settling cheap), not just an invitation**
8. "You owe us nothing until we recover compensation" (dolmanlaw.com) — **pattern: CTA-adjacent risk-removal line placed right before/after the action button**
9. "Available 24/7" / "available to assist you 24 hours a day" (omarochoalaw.com) — **pattern: availability framing functions as a CTA enabler, telling the visitor there's no bad time to reach out**
10. "Contact a [practice-area] lawyer as soon as possible" (pmtxlaw.com FedEx subpage) — **pattern: urgency-driven CTA embedded at the end of educational/informational content**

Overall CTA phrasing pattern across this niche: every site leads with some form of "free case evaluation/review," almost always paired with a "no fee unless we win" / contingency statement either directly above or below the button. Phone numbers are treated as a CTA, not just contact info — several sites repeat the number inline in body copy with urgency framing ("call us right away," "don't wait").

## Top 10 value props and framing

1. **Settlement-amount proof, tagged by case type** ($6.7M wrongful death, $5M car accident, etc.) — lets the end customer match their own injury type to a number, making the proof feel personally relevant rather than abstract.
2. **Cumulative "billions/millions recovered" headline stat** ("$30 billion," "$1 billion," "$400+ million") — establishes scale and credibility before any other claim.
3. **"No Win, No Fee" / contingency guarantee, repeated multiple times per page** — the single most repeated value prop across every site; framed as removing all financial risk to the end customer.
4. **"Not a settlement mill" / anti-quick-settlement positioning** (Omar Ochoa, Dolman) — directly differentiates against the perception that PI firms just want a fast payout and move on, addressing the "will they actually fight for me" fear from Sub-task 2.
5. **24/7 availability** ("accidents and disputes don't follow business hours") — matches the urgency/no-good-time fear identified in Sub-task 2.
6. **"Treat you like family" / personalized service framing** ("Highly Personalized Service," "we treat every client like family") — directly answers the "will I be ignored" fear, the #1 theme from Sub-task 2's review analysis.
7. **Founder personal story as trust mechanism** (Dolman's father's accident and bad-lawyer experience: "I've been in your shoes... I devote my life to giving you the service I wish he had") — converts a generic "why choose us" into a specific, emotionally resonant origin story.
8. **Direct access to attorneys** ("personal cell phone access to the partner" — Dolman testimonials) — concrete proof point for the "communication/being kept updated" theme, the strongest signal from Sub-task 2.
9. **Step-by-step "how it works" process framing** (Morgan & Morgan's 3-step: Submit your claim, We take action, We fight for you) — reduces the perceived complexity/uncertainty of hiring a lawyer, addressing the "don't understand the process" fear.
10. **Spanish-speaking services called out explicitly** (Omar Ochoa) — a concrete accessibility signal relevant to NY/Houston/LA markets with large Spanish-speaking populations.

## Common ad hook structures (inferred from homepage/landing patterns; no FB ad data pulled for this niche per lean-scope budget)

Based on the repeated homepage patterns above, the implied ad hooks for this niche follow three structures:
- **(a) Settlement-number hook**: "[Case type] victims have recovered up to $[X]M. Find out what your case is worth — free evaluation."
- **(b) Risk-removal hook**: "No win, no fee. You pay nothing unless we recover compensation for you. Call now."
- **(c) Urgency + availability hook**: "Injured? Don't wait. We're available 24/7 — talk to a [city] [practice-area] lawyer today."
