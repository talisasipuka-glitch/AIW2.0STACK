# 07 — Money Math (Personal Injury Lawyers)

Synthesis from Sub-tasks 1 (agency case studies), 3/4 (firm case-result figures), and 5 (contingency-fee positioning). Currency: USD.

---

## Average ticket size

PI firms work on contingency, typically **33% to 40% of the settlement or verdict**. The dollar figures firms feature on their homepages (Sub-task 3/4: Dolman's $6.7M wrongful death, $5M car accident, $3.2M brain injury) are the high end of the range used for trust-building, not the typical case.

The bulk of a firm's case volume is everyday car accident and slip-and-fall claims. Across the target markets (New York, Houston, Los Angeles), a realistic blended planning figure for a **typical (non-catastrophic) settled case is roughly $20,000 to $35,000**. At a 33% contingency fee, that's **roughly $6,600 to $11,550 in attorney fees per signed, settled case**.

Catastrophic cases (the $1M+ ones featured in marketing) happen far less often but carry outsized fee value (a $1M settlement at 33% is $330,000 in fees from a single case) — these are the cases the marketing exists to attract, even though the bulk of case volume is the smaller, everyday claims.

## Estimated close rate per qualified lead

PI intake has two conversion steps: (1) a website visitor submits a "free case evaluation" form, and (2) the firm's intake team determines the case is viable and the prospect signs a retainer. Sub-task 4 showed PI hero forms run 5-7+ fields specifically to support this qualification step.

Industry intake data for this niche typically shows **only 20% to 30% of inbound case-evaluation submissions convert to a signed client** — many submissions are not viable cases (no injury, fault unclear, statute of limitations issues, etc.), which Sub-task 1's "12,572 PI attorneys in New York / 6,334 in Texas" saturation context helps explain: the market is flooded with both genuine and marginal inquiries. A working planning assumption is **1 signed case per 4-5 qualified website leads (20-25%)**.

## Leads needed per month for a meaningful outcome

To add **1 new signed case per month** (a meaningful, provable outcome for a small or solo PI practice), at a 20-25% sign rate, the firm needs roughly **4-5 qualified case-evaluation submissions per month** from the website.

## Money math headline

"If we get you 4-5 qualified case-evaluation leads a month, and you sign roughly 1 in 4-5 of them, that's 1 new case a month — worth roughly $6,600 to $11,500 in attorney fees on a typical settled case, before counting the occasional larger case that can be worth 10-50x that. The website pays for itself with a single signed case."
