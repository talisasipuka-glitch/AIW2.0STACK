# 07 — Money Math (Real Estate Agents)

Synthesis from Sub-tasks 1 (agency case studies) and 2 (reviews/transaction signals). Currency: USD.

---

## Average ticket size

US median home sale price across the target markets (New York, Houston, Los Angeles) realistically ranges from roughly **$350,000 (Houston)** to **$800,000+ (Los Angeles/New York)**, with a blended planning figure around **$450,000-$500,000**.

Standard buyer's-agent or listing-agent commission is **2.5-3% of sale price**. At a $450,000 sale and 2.5%, that's **$11,250 gross commission per closed transaction** (before any brokerage split — the agent's net share is typically 50-70% of that, so roughly **$5,600-$7,900 net per deal** to the agent).

## Estimated close rate per qualified lead

Industry rule of thumb for online/inbound leads (vs. referrals) is low — typically **2-5%** convert to a closed transaction within 6-12 months, reflecting real estate's long decision cycle (Sub-task 2 shows buyers explicitly "interview several agents" before committing). For a website-driven lead funnel with reasonable qualification (the "private search" / saved-search pattern from Sub-tasks 3-4), a working planning assumption of **1 closing per 15-20 qualified leads (5-7%)** is realistic for a responsive, story-led agent site.

## Leads needed per month for a meaningful outcome

To add **1 extra closing every 2 months** (a meaningful, provable outcome for a solo agent or small team), at a 5-7% close rate, the agent needs roughly **8-10 new qualified leads per month** from the website.

## Money math headline

"If we get you 8-10 qualified buyer or seller leads a month, and you close roughly 1 in 15 of them, that's an extra closing every 2 months — worth around $11,000 in commission on an average $450,000 home. The website pays for itself with a single closing."
