## 2. Context bucket (paste as one text item)

```
Intake Agency is a web design and conversion agency built for one niche
only: personal injury law firms. The founder, Talisa Sipuka, works with
US-based PI firms from South Africa, building sites end to end with a
specialist team and process rather than spreading attention across
roofers, dentists, and law firms like a generalist agency would.

The core offer: a free homepage build, shown to the firm before they pay
anything. If they love it, they move forward on the full site. If not,
they walk. No deposit, no risk, no obligation.

The full build covers seven case-type landing pages (car accident, truck
accident, motorcycle, slip and fall, wrongful death, dog bite, brain
injury), each targeting a distinct high-intent search. Every page carries
LegalService, Attorney, and FAQPage schema so the firm shows up in both
Google and AI search results.

The trust stack is the differentiator. Research across the niche shows
the same pattern on every high-converting PI site: a No Win No Fee promise
placed near every call-to-action (not buried in the footer), a
settlement-dollar figure tagged by case type so an injured visitor can
match it to their own situation, and a named attorney with a real photo
and a direct-access promise, because the #1 fear after signing with a
firm is being ignored.

On conversion: most competitor sites run a 5 to 7 field intake form.
Intake Agency caps every form at 4 fields (name, phone, email, one-line
case description), with the No Win No Fee line directly above the form
and a privacy assurance directly below it. A sticky "Call Now, Open 24/7"
button sits in the header on every page for visitors who will never fill
out a form.

Delivery: 7 days from brand lock to live site, backed by a 100% money-back
guarantee if the deadline is missed. Pricing runs in three tiers: a
homepage-only build ($1,500 to $2,500), the anchor full-site build with
the complete trust stack ($4,000 to $6,000), and a full build plus an
ongoing content and SEO retainer ($6,000 plus $500 to $800 per month).

Status as of June 2026: first client live (Bricks Law, a solo-attorney PI
firm). Site and sales proposal both deployed. Too early for conversion
data, this is the proof point Intake Agency will build on.
```

## 3. Instructions bucket (paste as one text item, hard rules)

```
1. Every post speaks to a personal injury law firm owner or managing
   partner, never to an accident victim. The audience is a business owner
   deciding who builds their website, not someone who was just in a crash.
2. Every post ties back to the free homepage offer. The call to action is
   always some version of: comment HOMEPAGE and I will build yours for
   free, no obligation.
3. Never give legal advice. Never comment on case outcomes, settlement
   amounts for real cases, or anything that could be read as practicing
   law. Settlement figures are discussed only as a marketing pattern
   (proof-tagging on a website), never as legal guidance.
4. Never use em-dashes. Use periods, commas, or separate sentences instead.
5. Never use emojis.
6. No buzzwords. Banned words include leverage, synergy, robust, seamless,
   game-changer, cutting-edge, elevate, unlock, supercharge.
7. Tone is calm, direct, and professional. Plain words, short sentences,
   one idea per sentence. No hype, no fake urgency.
8. Always name the specific framework or pattern being taught (the trust
   stack, the 4-field form, the settlement-proof bar, the named-attorney
   story, the case-type landing page structure). Never speak in vague
   generalities like "good design matters."
9. Use real numbers from the research wherever possible (4-field form vs
   5 to 7 field competitor norm, 7 case-type pages, 7-day build timeline,
   under 3 second mobile load target).
10. Do not claim results, client counts, or testimonials Intake Agency
    does not yet have. As of June 2026 there is one client (Bricks Law,
    live, no analytics yet). Speak in terms of research findings and the
    build process, not in terms of "our clients see X% more calls" until
    that data exists.
11. Every script should be usable by someone with no on-camera setup. Talk
    to camera, voiceover over screen recordings of real PI firm websites
    (Talisa's own research captures), or text-on-screen formats.
12. When critiquing a real firm's website on camera, keep it factual and
    constructive (missing trust signal, form too long, no settlement
    number near the CTA). Never mock the firm or attorney by name in a way
    that could be seen as unprofessional or defamatory.
13. South Africa-to-US remote work is part of the story when relevant
    (time zone discipline, async communication, why a remote specialist
    can move faster than a local generalist agency), but it is a supporting
    detail, not the main hook.
14. The "no portfolio yet" stage is not hidden. If asked directly, frame it
    honestly: first client just shipped, free homepage offer exists
    specifically because of this stage.
15. Every script's hook must reference a specific, visible problem on a
    real PI firm homepage (missing settlement number, long form, buried No
    Win No Fee line, stock-photo gavel instead of a named attorney). Avoid
    generic hooks like "Is your website costing you clients?"
16. Comment-keyword CTA (HOMEPAGE) appears once per script, near the end,
    stated plainly: "Comment HOMEPAGE and I'll build yours for free."
17. Keep scripts grounded in the personal injury niche specifically. Do not
    generalize advice to "any law firm" or "any local business" unless the
    point is explicitly that PI is different from other niches.
18. When referencing competitor or top-of-niche sites (Morgan & Morgan,
    Dolman Law, Perdue & Kidd, etc.), describe only what is publicly visible
    on their site (layout, copy, structure). Never claim insider knowledge
    of their results or strategy.
19. Currency references default to USD, since the client base is US PI
    firms, even though Talisa is based in South Africa.
```

## 5. expert_brain bucket (paste each as a separate text item)

```
Item 1: The PI Trust Stack (Intake Agency research, 2026)
Why it matters: the single most important framework for this niche. Every
high-converting PI firm site stacks these in the same order: No Win No Fee
near every CTA, settlement-dollar proof tagged by case type, then a named
attorney with a direct-access promise. Name this framework explicitly in
any script that critiques or rebuilds a PI firm homepage.
```

```
Item 2: The 4-Field Form Pattern (Intake Agency research, 2026)
Why it matters: most PI competitor sites run a 5 to 7 field intake form.
Capping the form at 4 fields (name, phone, email, one-line case
description) with a No Win No Fee line above it and a privacy line below
it is a documented conversion pattern. Use this as a concrete, specific
example of a small change with an outsized effect.
```

```
Item 3: Settlement-Proof Tagging by Case Type (Intake Agency research, 2026)
Why it matters: a generic "we've recovered millions" stat does not work as
well as a settlement figure tagged to the specific case type (car
accident, truck accident, wrongful death, etc.), because an injured
visitor matches the number to their own situation. This is a reusable
teaching point about specificity over abstraction in trust signals.
```

```
Item 4: Case-Type Landing Page SEO Structure (Intake Agency research, 2026)
Why it matters: PI firms that rank well build 7 separate landing pages, one
per major case type (car accident, truck accident, motorcycle, slip and
fall, wrongful death, dog bite, brain injury), each carrying LegalService,
Attorney, and FAQPage schema. A firm with one generic "personal injury
lawyer" page is leaving high-intent searches on the table. Good material
for a "one page vs seven pages" comparison script.
```

```
Item 5: The Named-Attorney / Direct-Access Promise (Intake Agency research,
2026)
Why it matters: across customer reviews in this niche, the recurring
language is "they kept me updated," "they explained every step," "they
treated me like family." The #1 fear after signing with a firm is being
ignored. A named attorney with a real photo and an explicit communication
promise directly answers that fear. Useful for scripts about what PI
clients actually say in reviews versus what firm websites actually show.
```

```
Item 6: Sticky Call-Now, Open 24/7 (Intake Agency research, 2026)
Why it matters: some visitors will never fill out a form. A sticky header
"Call Now" link paired with an "Open 24/7" badge, visible on mobile,
captures that segment. Pairs well with the 4-field form item as "two
different visitors, two different paths to contact."
```

```
Item 7: The Free-Homepage, No-Portfolio Offer Shape (Intake Agency offer
pack, 2026)
Why it matters: at the no-portfolio stage, the offer is "I design your
homepage for free, you only pay if you love it." This is the founding
mechanic behind every Intake Agency CTA right now. Useful for scripts that
explain why the offer exists and why it is low-risk for the firm owner.
```

```
Item 8: Morgan & Morgan and Dolman Law as Structure References (public
sites, referenced in Intake Agency research, 2026)
Why it matters: these two firms' public homepages are repeatedly cited in
the research as examples of the trust stack done well (settlement proof,
press band, named-attorney story). Useful as on-screen examples when
walking through what "good" looks like in this niche. Describe only what
is publicly visible; do not claim insider knowledge.
```
