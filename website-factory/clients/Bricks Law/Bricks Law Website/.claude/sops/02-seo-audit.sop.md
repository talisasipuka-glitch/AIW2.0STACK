# 3 - SEO Audit

Implements: Stage 3.

## Purpose

Produce the local SEO and keyword strategy that Stage 5 (strategy) and
Stage 10.2 (personalize) will execute against, anchored on the
case-type-first keyword pattern this niche's SERPs reward.

## Inputs (per-client)

```
clients/[Client Name]/Pipeline Data/research/
```

## Procedure

1. Build the client's primary keyword cluster using the
   `"[case type] lawyer [city]"` pattern as the dominant formula, per
   `06-seo-landscape.md`. For each of the 7 case types the client covers
   (per Stage 1 intake), generate the corresponding keyword: "car accident
   lawyer [city]", "truck accident lawyer [city]", and so on.
2. Add the secondary, broader cluster: `"personal injury lawyer [city]"` and
   `"personal injury attorney near me"`. These are higher-competition and
   typically dominated by large multi-attorney firms and directories
   (Super Lawyers, Justia); record this as expected, not a failure.
3. Add long-tail clusters: `"[city] + free consultation"`, "no win no fee
   [city]", and case-type + injury-type combinations (burn injury, brain
   injury, wrongful death).
4. Confirm the title/H1 formula for the homepage:
   `"[Firm Name]: Personal Injury Lawyers [City/State]"` (brand-first,
   category-second, per `06-seo-landscape.md`).
5. Confirm the title/H1 formula for each case-type page:
   `"[Case Type] Lawyer in [City] | [Firm Name]"` (per
   `09-template-spec.md` Section 5 and `06-seo-landscape.md`'s
   case-type-specialist pattern).
6. Plan the schema markup set for Stage 10.2: `Attorney`, `LegalService`,
   `LocalBusiness` (with `openingHours` reflecting the confirmed hours from
   Stage 1, including "Open 24 hours" only if true), `AggregateRating` /
   `Review` from the client's GBP rating and review count, `FAQPage` on the
   homepage FAQ and every case-type page's FAQ block, and `BreadcrumbList`
   on all 7 case-type pages.
7. Identify the PAA (People Also Ask) question set for the homepage FAQ:
   cost of hiring a lawyer, average settlement amounts, "is it worth suing,"
   timeline, and partial-fault scenarios, per `06-seo-landscape.md`'s
   content gap analysis. Hand these to Stage 6 as required FAQ entries.
8. For each case type, identify at least one case-type-specific FAQ (e.g.
   "What if the other driver has no insurance?" for car accidents) per
   `09-wireframe.md`'s `CaseTypeLandingPage` FAQ requirement.

## Pass criteria

- A keyword list exists covering all 7 case types in the
  `"[case type] lawyer [city]"` pattern, plus the broader and long-tail
  clusters.
- Title/H1 formulas are confirmed for the homepage and all 7 case-type
  pages.
- The full schema markup plan (Attorney, LegalService, LocalBusiness,
  AggregateRating/Review, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList) is documented with the
  specific brand-dna fields each schema type will read.
- At least 5 PAA-driven FAQ questions are identified for the homepage FAQ,
  and at least 1 case-type-specific FAQ question per case type.

## When this stage halts

- The client covers fewer than 2 of the 7 case types (per Stage 1 intake).
  Flag to the student: the case-type-page SEO strategy is the template's
  primary differentiator and a near-empty case-type set changes the
  strategy materially.
- City/location data from Stage 1 is missing or ambiguous (e.g. firm serves
  a metro area with no single clear city). Confirm the primary city to
  target before generating keyword lists.

## Niche-specific notes

For this niche, the following pattern applies (derived from
`research/02-niche-research/personal-injury-lawyers/09-template-spec.md` +
the niche playbook):

Per `06-seo-landscape.md`, case-type-specific pages outrank generic
"personal injury lawyer [city]" pages for case-specific searches, and this
is the clearest content gap in the niche. The audit's primary output is not
a single "improve rankings" list, it is a confirmed 1:1 mapping of the 7
case-type pages to their target keyword and title/H1 formula, ready for
Stage 5 to slot into the sitemap and Stage 10.2 to inject as schema and
meta tags. "Open 24 hours" should only be encoded into `LocalBusiness`
schema if Stage 1 confirmed it is true for this specific client; do not
inherit it as a niche default.
