# 4 - Asset Harvest

Implements: Stage 4.

## Purpose

Collect the client's brand assets: logo, attorney headshots, office photos,
case-result graphics, trust badges, and any existing testimonial photos.
Halt the pipeline if no logo is found, per the universal asset-scraping
skill.

## Inputs (per-client)

```
clients/[Client Name]/Pipeline Data/research/
clients/[Client Name]/Pipeline Data/intake/
```

## Procedure

1. Scrape the client's existing website (if any) and Google Business
   Profile for: logo, attorney headshots (one per named attorney from Stage
   1), office exterior/interior photos, and any case-result graphics
   (settlement announcement images, news clippings).
2. Scrape linked social channels (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn) for
   additional attorney photos, team photos, and client testimonial photos
   where the client has posted them with permission.
3. Collect trust badges and affiliation marks: state bar logos, "Million
   Dollar Advocates Forum" or similar membership badges, "as seen in" press
   logos, Better Business Bureau or local "Best of [City]" badges, per
   `05-trust-signals.md`.
4. Explicitly DO NOT harvest or accept generic gavel, scales-of-justice, or
   courthouse-column stock imagery. Per `09-template-spec.md` Section 9 and
   `08-starter-template.md`, this imagery is a flagged anti-pattern for this
   niche. If the only "case type" imagery available is this kind of stock
   photo, flag it for Stage 5/Stage 9 rather than passing it through as a
   usable asset.
5. Sort harvested images into: logo, attorney headshots (tagged by attorney
   name), office photos, case-result graphics, trust badges/press logos,
   testimonial photos (tagged by client name and case type where known).
6. Cross-check against the case-type list from Stage 1: if a case type has
   a settlement figure but no associated case-result graphic, note the gap
   for Stage 9 (hero image) or Stage 6 (copy deck can present the figure as
   text-only).

## Pass criteria

- Logo found and saved. This is a hard requirement; see halt conditions.
- At least one attorney headshot per named attorney from Stage 1, or a
  flagged gap with a plan (Stage 9 generates a placeholder, or a photo
  shoot is scheduled before launch).
- Office photos collected if available.
- Trust badges and press logos collected and tagged.
- No gavel/scales-of-justice/courthouse stock imagery passed through as a
  primary asset.

## When this stage halts

- No logo found anywhere (site, GBP, social). This halts the entire
  pipeline per the universal asset-scraping skill. Escalate to the student
  to obtain or commission a logo before continuing.
- No attorney headshot exists for ANY named attorney and the client has not
  scheduled a photo shoot. Flag this strongly: per `05-trust-signals.md`,
  a named, photographed attorney is the single strongest humanizing trust
  element in this niche, and Stage 9's generated hero image will be weaker
  without a real photo to anchor it.

## Niche-specific notes

For this niche, the following pattern applies (derived from
`research/02-niche-research/personal-injury-lawyers/09-template-spec.md` +
the niche playbook):

Imagery direction for this niche is people-centric, not object-centric.
Prioritize: named attorneys with faces, client testimonial photos tied to a
case type and outcome, and real office photos that read as approachable
rather than imposing. Case-result graphics (a settlement announcement, a
news clipping, a "Results We Get" style card) are valuable if they exist.
Gavel and scales-of-justice imagery is an explicit anti-pattern; if a client
sends over a stock photo library that is mostly this kind of imagery,
sort it into a "not used" folder rather than the main asset manifest.
