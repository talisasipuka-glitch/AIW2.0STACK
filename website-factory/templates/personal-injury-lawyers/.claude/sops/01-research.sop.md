# 2 - Research

Implements: Stage 2.

## Purpose

Build a current picture of the client's online presence, reputation, and
local competitive position: Google Business Profile data, social channels,
and the firms competing for the same searches. This stage feeds Stage 3
(SEO audit) and Stage 5 (strategy) with facts, not assumptions.

## Inputs (per-client)

```
clients/[Client Name]/Pipeline Data/intake/
```

## Procedure

1. Pull the client's Google Business Profile via Apify
   (`compass/crawler-google-places`): name, address, phone, hours, category,
   review count, star rating, photos, and the "Open 24 hours" flag if
   present. Per `06-seo-landscape.md`, "Open 24 hours" is a near-universal
   GBP signal in this niche; record whether the client's GBP matches or
   diverges from that pattern.
2. Pull the most recent reviews (10 to 20) with text. Per
   `02-customer-voice.md`, the dominant themes to look for are
   communication/responsiveness ("kept me updated"), being treated like a
   person not a case number, and outcome satisfaction. Tag any review that
   mentions a case type and a dollar figure or outcome: these are
   testimonial candidates for Stage 6.
3. Identify 3 to 5 local competitor firms for the client's city using the
   same GBP search the client would be found under (e.g. "personal injury
   lawyer [city]" and "[case type] lawyer [city]"). Record their review
   counts, ratings, and whether they advertise "no win, no fee," 24/7
   availability, and bilingual service.
4. Pull the client's social channels (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn if
   present) for any existing case-result posts, attorney photos, or client
   testimonial content that could feed Stage 4 (asset scraper) or Stage 6
   (copy deck).
5. Note any existing press mentions, awards, or bar association
   affiliations. Per `05-trust-signals.md`, "as seen in" press logos and
   memberships like "Million Dollar Advocates Forum" function as a
   secondary trust layer; record anything the client already has before
   Stage 6 invents placeholder claims.
6. Log every Apify run to `logs/apify-runs.jsonl` per the factory's logging
   convention.

## Pass criteria

- GBP record captured: name, address, phone, hours, category, rating, review
  count, "Open 24/7" status.
- At least 5 recent reviews captured with text, tagged for testimonial
  candidacy where relevant.
- 3 to 5 local competitors identified with their headline trust claims
  (no win/no fee, 24/7, bilingual, press logos).
- Any existing press/award/affiliation assets identified and recorded for
  Stage 4 handoff.

## When this stage halts

- The client's GBP listing cannot be found or is unclaimed. Flag to the
  student: this affects both `LocalBusiness` schema accuracy (Stage 10.2)
  and the review-count trust signal (Stage 6).
- Apify actor returns zero results for the competitor search. Retry once
  with a broader query before flagging.

## Niche-specific notes

For this niche, the following pattern applies (derived from
`research/02-niche-research/personal-injury-lawyers/09-template-spec.md` +
the niche playbook):

Competitor research in this niche should specifically check for: the "Open
24/7" GBP flag (universal per `06-seo-landscape.md`), the presence of a
named attorney in the GBP photos or profile (versus a generic firm logo),
and whether competitors run case-type-specific landing pages (the SEO gap
`06-seo-landscape.md` identifies as the template's primary differentiator).
If most local competitors lack case-type pages, record this explicitly:
it is the headline competitive argument Stage 13 (proposal) will make.
