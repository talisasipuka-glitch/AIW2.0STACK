# 10.3 - Uplift

Implements: Stage 10.3.

## Purpose

Apply final niche-specific visual polish: the chevron shape motif, motion
refinement, and a verification pass on `tabular-nums` for every dollar
figure on the site.

## Inputs (per-client)

```
clients/[Client Name]/[Client Name] Website/dist/
templates/personal-injury-lawyers/niche-playbook/
```

## Procedure

1. Apply the chevron shape motif consistently across the locations
   specified in `09-template-spec.md` Section 1: corner overlays on hero
   and CTA band backgrounds (accent-yellow, 0.08 opacity), divider shapes
   between sections, and card-edge accents on PracticeAreasGrid cards and
   StatsBar figures. Apply with restraint, do not let the motif appear on
   every section.
2. Verify the restrained motion preset is applied consistently:
   `--motion-duration: 600ms`, `--motion-stagger: 90ms` across all animated
   sections (Hero, ProcessSteps, StatsBar, PracticeAreasGrid,
   Testimonials). Confirm `prefers-reduced-motion` disables all motion.
3. Run a tabular-nums verification pass: every dollar figure on the site
   (StatsBar entries on the homepage, single-figure StatsBar on each
   case-type page, any dollar amounts in testimonials or the
   PracticeAreasGrid cards) must render with `font-variant-numeric:
   tabular-nums`. This includes figures injected from
   `pages.services.items[].settlementFigure`.
4. Verify the palette restraint holds across the full build, not just the
   hero: bright yellow accent appears only on CTAs, dollar figures, and the
   "OPEN 24/7" badge (if applicable). Check the PracticeAreasGrid,
   StatsBar, and CTABand specifically, since these are the sections most
   likely to over-apply the accent color.
5. Verify section-to-section transitions use the light grey / white
   alternation described in `09-template-spec.md` Section 1, rather than
   hard borders.

## Pass criteria

- Chevron motif present at the specified locations (hero/CTA corner
  overlays, dividers, card edges), applied with restraint (not on every
  section).
- Motion preset consistent at 600ms duration / 90ms stagger across all
  animated sections; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored.
- Every dollar figure on every page (homepage StatsBar, case-type page
  single-figure StatsBar, any testimonial or grid dollar mentions) has
  `tabular-nums` applied.
- Yellow accent restraint holds across PracticeAreasGrid, StatsBar, and
  CTABand: no large yellow background fields.
- Section alternation uses light grey/white, no hard borders.

## When this stage halts

- Any dollar figure on any page (including case-type pages) renders without
  `tabular-nums`. This is a hard requirement, not a style suggestion, given
  how central settlement figures are to this niche's trust signal #2.
- The chevron motif or yellow accent is over-applied to the point of
  breaking the "restraint" requirement (e.g. a full-section yellow
  background). Scale back before passing to Stage 10.4a.

## Niche-specific notes

For this niche, the following pattern applies (derived from
`research/02-niche-research/personal-injury-lawyers/09-template-spec.md` +
the niche playbook):

The chevron motif was chosen specifically to evoke "forward motion" and
"fighting for you" without falling into the gavel/scales-of-justice cliche
(`08-starter-template.md`). Its restrained application (low-opacity corner
overlays, not full-section treatments) is what gives the reference site
(Morgan & Morgan, scored 12.5/12.5 on visual coherence) its coherence; an
uplift pass that over-applies the motif works against the reference it is
modeled on. Tabular-nums on dollar figures is a craft signal this niche's
premium-positioned firms rely on; the verification pass in this stage is
the last checkpoint before design-fidelity QA.
