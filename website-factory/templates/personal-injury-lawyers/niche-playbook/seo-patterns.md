# SEO Patterns, Personal Injury Lawyers

Written to unblock Stage 3 (SEO audit) and Stage 5 (strategy) of `/run-factory`.
Referenced by `.claude/skills/seo/SKILL.md` ("How this skill is customised"). Synthesized
from `research/02-niche-research/personal-injury-lawyers/` and the niche playbook already
generated by Module 2D (`trust-signals.json`, `vocabulary.json`, `copywriting.md`).

## Primary keyword templates

- Homepage: `personal injury lawyer [primary city]`, `personal injury attorney [primary
  city]`
- Service pages, one per case type from `research-extensions.md`'s
  `caseTypesHandled` enum:
  - `car accident lawyer [city]`
  - `truck accident lawyer [city]`
  - `motorcycle accident lawyer [city]`
  - `slip and fall lawyer [city]`
  - `wrongful death lawyer [city]`
  - `dog bite lawyer [city]`
  - `brain injury lawyer [city]`
  - `medical malpractice lawyer [city]`
  - `workers compensation lawyer [city]`
- Location pages: `personal injury lawyer [city name]`, `[city name] accident attorney`

## LSI keyword themes

Use naturally across body copy, never stuffed:

- "no win no fee" / "no fee unless we win" / "contingency fee"
- "free case evaluation" / "free consultation"
- "settlement" / "verdict" / "compensation" / "damages"
- "insurance claim" / "insurance company" / "claims adjuster"
- "statute of limitations" (Georgia personal injury, 2 years)
- "medical bills" / "lost wages" / "pain and suffering"
- "accident report" / "police report"
- "se habla espanol" (only if `nicheExtensions.personal-injury-lawyers.bilingualServices`
  includes Spanish)

## Schema subtype

Primary: `schema.org/Attorney` (a subtype of `LocalBusiness` and `LegalService`). Use
`Attorney` on the homepage and location pages for NAP + `areaServed` +
`AggregateRating`. Use `LegalService` on service/case-type pages with
`Service.serviceType` set to the case type (e.g. "Car Accident Representation").

`FAQPage` schema mandatory on every service page (5-8 Q&As). `Article` schema on blog
posts. `BreadcrumbList` on every page.

## Average job value (for revenue-impact math)

Use **$6,000** (USD) as the conservative average value of a signed personal injury case
to the firm (attorney fee on a contingency basis, blended across smaller soft-tissue
claims and larger litigated cases). This is intentionally conservative, actual average
case values vary widely by case-type mix and should be replaced with the client's own
figures if they provide them during onboarding. Never invent a higher number for the
audit/proposal math.

## Conversion-rate baseline

- Website visitor to lead (free-consultation form or call): **3%**
- Lead to signed case: **30%** (industry-typical intake-to-retainer rate for PI firms)

For Stage 3 revenue-impact math, use the 3% visitor-to-lead rate against the traffic
gap. Do not compound through to signed-case rate unless the client specifically asks
for a "cases signed per month" projection, in which case multiply leads by 30%.

## Common pitfalls and doorway-page risks specific to this niche

- **Attorney advertising rules**: Most state bars (including Georgia) require
  disclaimers on results pages, e.g. "Results do not guarantee a similar outcome" near
  settlement/verdict figures. Every settlement-results section must carry this
  disclaimer.
- **Never promise outcomes**: avoid "we will win your case" / "guaranteed settlement"
  language. "No Win, No Fee" describes the fee structure, not a result guarantee, keep
  those concepts separate in copy.
- **AggregateRating schema must be real**: only emit `AggregateRating` if the client has
  a verifiable review count/rating (Google or a third-party directory). Do not fabricate
  or round up.
- **Location pages must reflect real presence**: do not generate a location page implying
  a physical office in a city where the firm has no office, unless the page is clearly
  framed as a service area (per the SEO skill's location-page rules: "[Service] in
  [City]" framed around service area, not a fake office address). If a client's mailing
  address is a P.O. Box (as flagged for this client), do not emit a street address in
  `LocalBusiness`/`Attorney` schema until a real address is confirmed.
- **Case-type doorway pages**: each case-type page must contain genuinely different
  content (process specifics, common injuries, typical timeline, FAQs unique to that
  case type), not a templated swap of the case-type name.
